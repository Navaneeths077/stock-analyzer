📊 Understanding Valuation Metrics

P/E Ratio: Price per rupee of earnings. Lower = Cheaper. 2.83 means ₹2.83 per ₹1 of profit.

Book Value: Asset value per share. P/B of 0.81 means trading below assets.

Dividend Yield: Annual cash return. 0% means all profits reinvested.

EPS: Earnings per share. Higher EPS = Better profitability.

💰 Understanding Profitability Metrics

ROE (25.9%): For every ₹100 of equity, profit is ₹25.90. Top 5% when > 20%.

ROCE (20.5%): Returns on invested capital. > 15% = Excellent efficiency.

Margins: Operating & Net margins show % of revenue as profit.

📈 Understanding Growth Metrics

Revenue Growth: Annual sales increase. 21.4% is 2-4x market average.

CAGR: Compound Annual Growth Rate over 3-5 years. Shows sustained growth.

Profit Growth: Earnings expansion. Should match or exceed revenue growth.

🏦 Understanding Financial Health

Debt-to-Equity: Leverage level. 0.12 = Virtually debt-free. < 1.0 is safe.

Current Ratio: Short-term liquidity. > 1.5 means healthy cash position.

Reserves: Cash/savings accumulated. More = Better flexibility.

🎯 Investment Ratings

STRONG BUY (75+): Exceptional opportunity with strong fundamentals.

BUY (60-74): Good company with attractive metrics.

HOLD (45-59): Mixed signals, wait for clarity.

WEAK SELL (30-44): Concerning metrics, limited upside.
